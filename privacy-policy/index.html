Privacy Policy

We are committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your personal information.

Last Updated: 28 January 2026

1. Information We Collect

  • Service Providers: Third-party companies that provide services on our behalf, such as payment processing, website hosting, email marketing, and analytics. These service providers are contractually obligated to protect your information and use it only for the purposes for which we disclose it to them.  

  • Reservation Details: When you make a reservation through our Website, we collect specific details about your booking, such as the intended date and time of your visit, the number of people in your party, and any special requests you may have (e.g., dietary restrictions, seating preferences).

  • Business Transfers: In the event of a merger, acquisition, or other corporate reorganization, your information may be transferred to the acquiring entity.  


2. How We Use Your Information


Legal Requirements: We may disclose your personal information to law enforcement agencies, regulatory bodies, or other governmental authorities if we are legally compelled to do so, or if we believe in good faith that such disclosure is necessary to comply with applicable law, to protect our rights or property, or to protect the safety and security of our users or the public. With Your Consent: In situations not specifically outlined in this Privacy Policy, we will only share your personal information with third parties if we have obtained your explicit and informed consent to do so.

  • Processing and confirming your reservations.

  • Responding to your inquiries and requests.

  • Sending you reservation confirmations and updates.

  • Sending you promotional emails and newsletters (if you have opted in).

  • Personalizing your experience on our Website.


3. How We Share Your Information

  • Service Providers: Third-party companies that provide services on our behalf, such as payment processing, website hosting, email marketing, and analytics. These service providers are contractually obligated to protect your information and use it only for the purposes for which we disclose it to them.  


  • Business Transfers: In the event of a merger, acquisition, or other corporate reorganization, your information may be transferred to the acquiring entity.  


  • Legal Requirements: We may disclose your information if required to do so by law or in response to a valid legal request, such as a court order or government investigation.  


  • With Your Consent: We may share your information with third parties with your explicit consent.


4. Data Security

We take reasonable measures to protect your personal information from unauthorized access, use, disclosure, alteration, or destruction. These measures include:

  • Using secure server environments.

  • Implementing encryption technologies (such as SSL/TLS) to protect sensitive information transmitted online.

  • Restricting access to your personal information to authorized personnel who need to know the information to perform their job duties.  


    However, please remember that no method of transmission over the internet or method of electronic storage is completely secure. Therefore, while we strive to use commercially acceptable means to protect your personal information, we cannot guarantee its absolute security.
